Robot 1: Shooter Bot

In 2025, the game switched to basketball-and so did our robot design. R1 for this year was built to score. Its shooting system used a dual flywheel setup that could control both the angle and speed of the shot. Whether it was a 2-pointer or a 3-pointer, the robot handled it with precision. To make shots accurate, we used an AI-based auto-aiming system. A camera detected the hoop, and the Jetson Nano calculated the best angle for shooting. This allowed the bot to score consistently from different positions. A special catcher ensured smooth ball handling during fast movements. The entire bot was designed to be fast, stable, and accurate.

Robot 2: Assist & Defense Bot

R1-Robocon 2024: Manual Farming Bot

Our first robot from 2024 was a manually controlled farming bot designed for planting saplings and transferring grains across the arena. It was operated using a custom wireless app, giving the driver smooth control throughout the match. Toplant saplings, the robot used two pneumatic claws that could pick up and place two saplings at once. These were lifted and lowered using a belt and pulley system powered by stepper motors, allowing accurate positioning every time. The gripping system was cushioned to hold saplings without damaging them. For grain transfer, we used a unique approach. Instead of moving grains from place to place manually, the robot launched them using compressed air and a lever-like mechanism. This reduced movement time and made the process fast and efficient, all while keeping the robot lightweight and responsive.

R2‚ÄìRobocon 2024: Autonomous Silo Bot

Our second bot from 2024 was fully autonomous and focused on picking grains and placing them into silos. It navigated the field using mecanum wheels, which let it move in any direction without turning. The bot used sensors to follow lines, avoid obstacles, and detect walls. It was equipped with cameras and laser sensors to locate grains and silos. Once it found a grain, a claw-lift system picked it up and dropped it into the correct silo. To make smart decisions, the robot used reinforcement learning. It followed a trained Q table that helped it decide which silo to go to next. With features like dead reckoning, perspective correction, and custom PID control, the robot adapted to different match conditions without manual help.
